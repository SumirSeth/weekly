Poems LAST RAYS

By Jahnavi Singh.

A tinge of nothingness in my heart, under the fading sunlight succumbs to the screams of the fading soul of desires , to be held in the arms till it's burning deep inside , to be held delicately for the stone is weathering under the fast moving winds, to be heard with equanimity in the cacophony of life's breathing urge. Albeit the morning is going to hold me upright, the moonrise brings me to this amalgamation of blues, longing for the distant pole star, for the shine ceases the sozzled self with the yearn of caress. The fading lights, bring me to the hostage of ivories for the resonance puts on the musings while the birds go back home alone. With the last rays, the last sound of my keys, and the shrunken soul ends up on its own shoulders. Alas, the distant diamond sky is up now.
